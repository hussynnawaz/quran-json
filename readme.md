## Introduction

The Quran consists of 114 Surahs, each varying in length and content. Understanding the background, meanings, and themes of each Surah is essential for Muslims seeking knowledge and spiritual enlightenment. This repository aims to provide detailed information about each Surah, including their Arabic and English names, meanings, and revelation places.

## Surah Meanings

The meanings of the Surahs vary, ranging from lessons in faith, guidance on ethical behavior, historical narratives, and reminders of divine power and mercy. Each Surah carries its unique message and significance.

## Surah Revelation Places

The Surahs in the Quran were revealed in two main locations: Mecca and Medina. Understanding the context of revelation provides insights into the themes and messages conveyed in each Surah.

- **Mecca:** Surahs revealed in Mecca often focus on fundamental aspects of faith, spirituality, and moral values. They address the challenges faced by early Muslims in Mecca, emphasizing perseverance, patience, and steadfastness in the face of adversity.

- **Medina:** Surahs revealed in Medina often deal with legal and social matters, providing guidance on governance, justice, and community relations. They reflect the evolving Muslim community in Medina and address specific situations and challenges faced by the early Muslim society.
